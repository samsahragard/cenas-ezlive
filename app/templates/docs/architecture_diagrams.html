Snapshot rule: potential_payout is computed via compute_one() at open_for_bidding / approved and frozen on the row, so future pay-formula changes don't retroactively alter quoted earnings. paid_payout is written only when the PayCheck closes.

3. Driver score → tier ladder

Rolling-30-day score (driver_scoring.py) snapshots to driver_score nightly at 4 AM CT, then writes drivers.current_score + current_tier. New drivers (< 20 lifetime deliveries) cap at New regardless of score.

score = 0.25 × tracking_pct + 0.25 × ontime_pct + 0.20 × (100 − cancellation_pct) + 0.10 × photo_pct + 0.10 × response_pct + 0.10 × stars_pct

10. ezCater payout formula at a glance

Per-delivery, from app/services/ezcater_payroll.py:compute_one. Tracked = driver opened a shift + streamed GPS through delivery.

base = $25 (always) tracked = $10 (if Tracking column ✓ in performance report) distance = $1.50 × max(0, pickup_miles − 20) (only if Tracked) five_star = $5 (if customer rated 5★, only if Tracked) ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────── payout = base + tracked + distance + five_star
Tracking is the gate. Without it the driver gets only the $25 base — distance, mileage, and 5★ bonuses don't apply. This is why Ez Market displays a "best-case" projection that assumes tracking on, and why the driver-app push for shift tracking matters.
