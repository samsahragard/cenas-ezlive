# Distilled Leadership Rules for Store Managers (Cena's Kitchen)
*Based on the 21 Irrefutable Laws of Leadership by John C. Maxwell*

This playbook provides actionable rules for GMs, KMs, and FOH Managers to guide decision-making, employee relationships, and operations.

---

### 1. The Law of the Lid (Effectiveness Limit)
* **Logic**: A manager's leadership ability is the "lid" on their shift/store's effectiveness. Smart systems fail without strong leadership.
* **Manager Action**: Focus on improving your personal leadership skills (listening, problem-solving, communication), not just managing tasks.

### 2. The Law of Influence (True Measure of Leadership)
* **Logic**: Leadership is influence—nothing more, nothing less. Titles or positions (e.g., "Shift Lead") do not guarantee people will follow.
* **Manager Action**: Earn influence by helping team members, showing competence, and listening. Don't rely on "because I'm the manager" to get compliance.

### 3. The Law of Process (Daily Development)
* **Logic**: Leadership is built daily, not overnight.
* **Manager Action**: Establish consistent daily habits: pre-shift briefings, real-time coaching, and daily self-evaluation.

### 4. The Law of Navigation (Chart the Course)
* **Logic**: Anyone can steer the ship, but it takes a leader to chart the course.
* **Manager Action**: Plan shifts in advance. Anticipate peak hours, table rushes, and prep demands before they happen.

### 5. The Law of Addition (Adding Value by Serving)
* **Logic**: Leaders add value by serving others.
* **Manager Action**: Help FOH/BOH teams during rushes (e.g., running food, busing tables, jumping on the prep line). Serve your staff so they can serve guests.

### 6. The Law of Solid Ground (Trust is the Foundation)
* **Logic**: Trust is the foundation of leadership. Character, competence, and consistency build trust.
* **Manager Action**: Be honest about mistakes, treat everyone fairly, and follow through on promises (like shift adjustments or reviews).

### 7. The Law of Respect (Following Stronger Leaders)
* **Logic**: People naturally follow leaders stronger than themselves.
* **Manager Action**: Show respect to your team first, handle high-stress rushes calmly, and demonstrate superior job competency.

### 8. The Law of Intuition (Leadership Bias)
* **Logic**: Evaluate operational trends and morale with a leadership bias.
* **Manager Action**: Read the floor. Look at team energy, ticket times, and customer faces, and make preemptive staff adjustments.

### 9. The Law of Magnetism (Attracting Who You Are)
* **Logic**: You attract who you are. High-performing, positive managers attract high-performing, positive staff.
* **Manager Action**: Model the work ethic, punctuality, and attitude you want to see on your shift.

### 10. The Law of Connection (Touch the Heart First)
* **Logic**: Leaders connect with people individually before asking them to execute tasks.
* **Manager Action**: Greet employees by name at clock-in, ask about their day, and build personal rapport before delegating heavy shift workloads.

### 11. The Law of the Inner Circle (Surrounding Team)
* **Logic**: A manager's success is determined by those closest to them (Shift Leads, Head Cooks, Lead Servers).
* **Manager Action**: Identify and mentor key leaders on your staff to act as your operational anchors.

### 12. The Law of Empowerment (Giving Power to Others)
* **Logic**: Only secure leaders delegate authority and empower others.
* **Manager Action**: Empower lead servers to handle minor guest complaints, and lead cooks to run prep checks without micromanaging.

### 13. The Law of the Picture (People Do What They See)
* **Logic**: Employees copy their manager's behavior.
* **Manager Action**: If you want employees to be on time, dress professionally, and treat guests with extreme courtesy, you must do it first.

### 14. The Law of Buy-In (Buy-In to the Leader)
* **Logic**: Staff must buy into you as a manager before they buy into your vision/rules.
* **Manager Action**: Prioritize building credibility and trust before rolling out major operational changes.

### 15. The Law of Victory (Finding a Way to Win)
* **Logic**: Leaders find a way for the team to succeed, refusing to accept failure.
* **Manager Action**: During chaotic rushes, coordinate staffing dynamically (e.g., cross-training bussers to host, moving prep cooks to the line) to ensure guest satisfaction.

### 16. The Law of the Big Mo (Momentum is Key)
* **Logic**: Operational momentum makes solving problems easier.
* **Manager Action**: Celebrate small wins (e.g., record low ticket times or a high sales day) to keep team morale high.

### 17. The Law of Priorities (Activity vs. Accomplishment)
* **Logic**: Activity is not necessarily accomplishment. Focus on high-impact tasks.
* **Manager Action**: Spend time on high-impact coaching and guest interactions rather than hiding in the office doing paperwork during peak hours.

### 18. The Law of Sacrifice (Giving Up to Go Up)
* **Logic**: Leadership requires sacrificing personal comfort for the team's success.
* **Manager Action**: Work unpopular shifts, stay late when someone calls out, and put the store's needs before your personal preferences.

### 19. The Law of Timing (When to Lead)
* **Logic**: When to take action is as important as what action to take.
* **Manager Action**: Address performance issues immediately after the shift (not during a rush). Roll out new menus during slow weekdays, not busy Friday nights.

### 20. The Law of Explosive Growth (Leading Leaders)
* **Logic**: To multiply results, develop other leaders (managers/shift leads), don't just instruct followers.
* **Manager Action**: Train your shift leads so they can eventually run shifts independently.

### 21. The Law of Legacy (Lasting Value)
* **Logic**: A manager's lasting value is measured by how well the store runs in their absence.
* **Manager Action**: Write clear operational checklists and train backups so the store runs perfectly even when you are off.
